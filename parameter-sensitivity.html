Parameter Sensitivity

Environmental conditions are rarely static or precisely known. A robust engineering model requires sensitivity analysis to understand which input parameters dominate the system's behavior.

We perturb the key independent variables by ±20% from their baseline values and measure the resulting change in the final state variables at t = 168 hours. The parameters of highest interest are:

  • Wind Speed (W) — directly drives evaporation mass transfer and wave-induced emulsification.
  • Temperature (T) — strongly dictates the evaporation rate.
  • Initial Volume (V_s) — dictates slick thickness and total spreading area.
  • Oil Density (ρ_o) — influences gravity-driven spreading.
