ODE Model Formulation

The core of the simulation is a system of coupled, non-linear Ordinary Differential Equations. Because these equations are tightly coupled (e.g., evaporation depends on area, area depends on volume, viscosity depends on evaporation), they must be solved simultaneously.

Note on Solvers: This system is stiff and highly coupled. In our numerical methods, we primarily rely on a 4th-order Runge-Kutta (RK4) implementation and MATLAB's adaptive ode45 to maintain stability.
Spreading Rate (dA/dt)
Governs the rate of change of the slick area over time. Derived from Fay's spreading theory for the gravity-viscous phase.
dA/dt = K_s * A^(1/3) * (V_s/A)^(4/3) * (v_w/A)^(1/3)

Variables Legend:

  • A= Slick area (m²)
  • K_s= Spreading constant
  • V_s= Volume of the spill (m³)
  • v_w= Kinematic viscosity of water
Evaporation Fraction (dF_E/dt)
Models the mass fraction of oil that has evaporated. Highly dependent on temperature and wind speed.
dF_E/dt = K_2 * A * (T_c * exp(B(1 - T_o/T)) / h)

Variables Legend:

  • F_E= Evaporated fraction (0 to 1)
  • K_2= Mass transfer coefficient
  • T= Environmental temperature (K)
  • T_o= Initial boiling point (K)
  • h= Slick thickness (V_s / A)
Water Incorporation / Emulsification (dY/dt)
Models the fractional water content incorporated into the oil slick due to wave action.
dY/dt = 2e-6 * (1 + 0.7)^2 * W^2 * (C_s - Y)

Variables Legend:

  • Y= Fractional water content
  • W= Wind speed (m/s)
  • C_s= Maximum water content (~0.7 to 0.8)
Viscosity Change
An algebraic relation describing how viscosity increases as lighter fractions evaporate.
μ = μ_0 * exp(C_d * F_E)

Variables Legend:

  • μ= Current dynamic viscosity
  • μ_0= Initial dynamic viscosity
  • C_d= Oil-specific viscosity constant
