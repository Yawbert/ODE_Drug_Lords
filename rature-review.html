Literature Review

The models implemented in this project are built upon decades of experimental and theoretical research into marine hydrodynamics and hydrocarbon physical chemistry.

Sebastiao, P. & Guedes Soares, C. (1995)
Modeling the Fate of Oil Spills at Sea
Spill Science & Technology Bulletin, Vol. 2(2/3), pp. 121–131.
Project Note: Primary reference. Provides the core ODE system structure.
Fay, J.A. (1969)
The Spread of Oil Slicks on a Calm Sea
Oil on the Sea, Plenum Press, New York.
Project Note: Foundational theory on the gravity-viscous phase of surface spreading.
Mackay, D., Buist, I., Mascarenhas, R., & Paterson, S. (1980)
A Computational Model for the Behavior of Oil Spills
Proc. 3rd Arctic Marine Oilspill Program Technical Seminar.
Project Note: Pioneering formulation of the emulsification and water incorporation rates.
Stiver, W. & Mackay, D. (1984)
Evaporation Rate of Spills of Hydrocarbons and Petroleum Mixtures
Environmental Science & Technology, Vol. 18, pp. 834–840.
Project Note: Empirical derivation of the evaporation fraction equations.
Delvigne, G.A.L. & Sweeney, C.E. (1988)
Natural Dispersion of Oil
Oil & Chemical Pollution, Vol. 4, pp. 281–310.
Project Note: Analysis of wave energy required to entrain oil droplets.
